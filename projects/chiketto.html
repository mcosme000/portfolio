Chiketto

About this project

This is one of the projects I made for the Google UX Design Specialization course. Our task was to design any app, starting from the user research, creating low fidelity prototypes, doing usability studies and finally create the the high fidelity prototypes.

I choose a cinema app because I thought it would be a good opportunity to practice designing the UI part (from movie or cast information pages) and the UX usability part (seat and tickets selection, different inputs, etc...)

Overview

There is a wide range of cinemas in Tokyo. Some are large companies, while others are local and more independent cinemas. This is both an advantage and a disadvantage, as the simple task of searching for a film can take more time than desired. This, coupled with the number of dedicated cinema apps that exist, but the lack of one that includes them all and also allows in-app payments, can lead to frustration for users.

The problem

There are too many cinema apps in Tokyo, but none of them have an in-app payment or language switch options.

The solution

The aim is to design a dedicated cinema app that allows in-app payments and the option to switch languages, while at the same time being intuitive for any type of user.

Role

  • UX/UI designer
  • User research and interviews
  • Low and high fidelity prototypes
  • Usability studies
  • Design implementation

Project duration

  • First part: Aug-Sep 2021
  • Second part: Jan-Feb 2022
