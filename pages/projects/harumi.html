About this project

This is the final project of the Google UX Design Specialization course. During the last few weeks we were learning about responsive design and how important it is to create designs that adapt to any screen size. Therefore, our final task was to create a website design in various formats.

In my case, I chose to make it about a bakery. Near my house there is a well known bakery where there are always people waiting, so it was my inspiration for this project.

Overview

Harumi Bakery is a local business in Setagaya Ward, Tokyo, that have been on business for 30 years. They offer different types of bread, but their cakes are their most famous product and the ones that sell out the fastest. Since they added these cakes to their menu, they have gained even more popularity and have been showcases in different travel and food magazines. Everyday there are a lot of customers waiting in line to buy those cakes, but not everybody is able to get one.

The problem

This bakery doesn't have any system that allows customers to place preorders. Because of that, they need to wait in line for long periods of time.

The solution

Design a fully functional and responsive website for the business that is intuitive for all types of customers and that allows them to easily check the menu and make preorders.

Role

  • UX/UI designer
  • User research and interviews
  • Low and high fidelity prototypes
  • Usability studies
  • Design implementation

Project duration

  • November - December 2021
